What is Coercive Control?

Coercive control is a pattern of behavior that seeks to dominate, isolate, and manipulate another person, often through psychological, emotional, and non-physical means. It is recognized as a serious form of abuse that can have lasting impacts on individuals and families.

Credible sources:

  • Duluth Power and Control Wheels – The Duluth Model is a widely recognized framework for understanding patterns of abuse and control.
  • Coercive Control: Update and Review – National Institutes of Health (NIH)
  • Women's Aid UK: Coercive Control
  • NDCC Chapter 12: North Dakota Century Code
  • National Domestic Violence Hotline

What is DARVO?

DARVO stands for Deny, Attack, and Reverse Victim and Offender. It is a common tactic used by abusers to deflect blame and further harm victims, making it difficult for survivors to seek justice and support.

Credible sources:

  • Dr. Jennifer Freyd: Defining DARVO – The researcher who coined the term DARVO.
  • Psychology Today: DARVO
  • DARVO in Institutional Betrayal – National Institutes of Health (NIH)
